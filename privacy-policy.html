Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your information when you use IGOE SCAN.

Last Updated: March 19, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to IGOE SCAN ("the Tool"), a free AI readiness scanner for medical practices, operated by Conscious Health Connections ("we," "us," or "our"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit and use this website.

By using the Tool, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Tool.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

  • Domain names you submit — the website URLs you enter into the scanner for analysis.
  • Name and email address — if you voluntarily provide them in the optional email capture form to receive your report.
  • Usage data — basic analytics such as pages visited, time on page, browser type, and device type, collected via standard web analytics tools.
  • Local storage data — scan history and preferences stored locally in your browser (never transmitted to our servers unless you submit your email).
Important: We do not scan, store, or retain any content from the websites you submit for analysis. The Tool fetches publicly available page data temporarily in your browser session only, for the sole purpose of generating your report.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To operate and improve the IGOE SCAN tool and its features.
  • To send you a copy of your scan report if you provide your email address.
  • To contact you about relevant services offered by Conscious Health Connections, if you have opted in.
  • To understand usage patterns and improve the user experience.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

4. Email Communications

If you provide your email address, we may send you your scan report and occasional marketing communications from Conscious Health Connections about AI visibility services for medical practices. You can unsubscribe from marketing emails at any time by clicking the "Unsubscribe" link in any email we send, or by contacting us directly.

We will never sell, rent, or share your email address with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

The Tool may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience. These may include:

  • Functional cookies — to remember your preferences and scan history within your browser session.
  • Analytics cookies — to understand how visitors use the Tool (e.g., Google Analytics or similar).

You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, some features of the Tool may not function properly without cookies.

6. Third-Party Services

The Tool uses third-party CORS proxy services to fetch publicly available website data for analysis. These services may log request metadata (such as the URL requested and IP address) in accordance with their own privacy policies. We use these services solely to enable the scanning functionality and do not share personal information with them.

We may also use third-party analytics providers. These providers may collect information about your use of the Tool in accordance with their own privacy policies.

7. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. HIPAA Notice

IGOE SCAN does not collect, process, or store any Protected Health Information (PHI). The Tool analyzes publicly available website data only. It is not a covered entity or business associate under HIPAA, and this service does not involve patient records or medical data of any kind.

9. Children's Privacy

The Tool is not directed to individuals under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately.

10. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • The right to access the personal information we hold about you.
  • The right to request correction of inaccurate information.
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information.
  • The right to opt out of marketing communications at any time.
  • The right to data portability (where applicable under GDPR or CCPA).

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the address below.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Your continued use of the Tool after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the new policy.
